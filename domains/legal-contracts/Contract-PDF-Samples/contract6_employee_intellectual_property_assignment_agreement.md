# EMPLOYEE INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT AGREEMENT

**Parties:**  
- **Company:** Memora Technologies Limited, a company incorporated in Hong Kong with its principal place of business at 18/F, Tower One, Lippo Centre, 89 Queensway, Admiralty, Hong Kong ("Company")  
- **Employee:** [Employee Name], an individual residing at [Address] ("Employee")  

**Governing Law:** Hong Kong Special Administrative Region  

---

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 This Agreement ensures that all intellectual property created by Employee in connection with Company business is properly assigned to Company, protecting Company's competitive position and patent strategy.

1.2 This Agreement applies to all inventions, designs, software, algorithms, documentation, and works of authorship created by Employee:
- During employment with Company;
- Using Company resources, equipment, or confidential information; or
- Relating to Company's products, services, or business activities.

## 2. DEFINITION OF WORK PRODUCT

2.1 "Work Product" means any and all inventions, discoveries, improvements, designs, software code, algorithms, documentation, trade secrets, know-how, and works of authorship, whether patentable or not, created by Employee alone or jointly with others during the term of employment.

2.2 Work Product specifically includes, but is not limited to:
- Improvements to the **hybrid storage architecture** (peripheral datastore on smart companion → primary datastore on link device → automatic erasure upon sync confirmation).
- Designs for **link devices without input/output elements** (no screen, buttons, ports, or speakers) for security purposes.
- Algorithms for **spaced repetition scheduling** integrated with persona models, optical sensor position tracking, or multimodal animation synchronization.
- Firmware for **PAN-triggered synchronization** (Bluetooth Low Energy / IEEE 802.15.4) with proximity-based authentication.
- Persona model structures (relationship stage, personality, mood, biographical, wellness components) and refinement logic.
- Any technical specifications, user interface designs, or manufacturing processes related to Memora Smart Companion (Model MC-110) or Link Device (Model LD-115).

## 3. ASSIGNMENT OF INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

3.1 Employee hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in and to all Work Product, including:
- All global patent rights, copyright, trade secret rights, mask work rights, and moral rights (to the extent waivable under applicable law).
- The right to file, prosecute, maintain, and enforce patent applications and registrations related to Work Product.
- All future rights, extensions, renewals, and divisions thereof.

3.2 Employee agrees that Work Product is "work made for hire" to the fullest extent permitted by law. To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as work made for hire, Employee hereby assigns such Work Product to Company as provided in Section 3.1.

## 4. DISCLOSURE AND ASSISTANCE

4.1 Employee shall promptly disclose in writing to Company all Work Product, providing sufficient detail to enable Company to evaluate and protect such Work Product.

4.2 Employee shall cooperate with Company, at Company's expense, to:
- Execute patent applications, assignments, and other documents necessary to perfect Company's rights in Work Product.
- Provide testimony, affidavits, or other assistance in patent prosecution, litigation, or enforcement proceedings.
- Maintain confidentiality of Work Product until Company elects to file for protection or publicly disclose.

4.3 These obligations survive termination of employment for any reason.

## 5. PRE-EXISTING INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

5.1 Employee represents that the list attached as **Exhibit A** contains all inventions, original works of authorship, developments, and improvements conceived, developed, or reduced to practice by Employee prior to employment with Company that Employee wishes to exclude from the definition of Work Product ("Excluded IP").

5.2 If no list is attached, Employee represents that there is no Excluded IP.

5.3 Employee grants Company a non-exclusive, royalty-free, worldwide license to use any Excluded IP that is incorporated into or necessary for Company products, solely for Company's internal business purposes.

## 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 Employee acknowledges that Company possesses valuable confidential information, including technical specifications, business strategies, customer data, and unpublished patent applications.

6.2 Employee agrees not to disclose or use Company confidential information except as required to perform duties for Company, both during and after employment.

6.3 These confidentiality obligations survive termination of employment indefinitely for trade secrets and for five (5) years for other confidential information.

## 7. RETURN OF MATERIALS

7.1 Upon termination of employment or Company's request, Employee shall promptly return all Company property, including documents, devices, prototypes, and confidential information, in any form.

7.2 Employee shall not retain copies of any Company materials except as required by law or with Company's prior written consent.

## 8. NO CONFLICTING OBLIGATIONS

8.1 Employee represents that execution of this Agreement and performance of duties for Company do not violate any prior agreements or obligations to third parties.

8.2 Employee shall not incorporate any third-party intellectual property into Work Product without Company's prior written authorization.

## 9. REMEDIES

9.1 Employee acknowledges that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to Company for which monetary damages may be insufficient.

9.2 Accordingly, Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, or other equitable remedies, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity.

## 10. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

10.1 Except for breaches of confidentiality, IP assignment, or indemnity obligations, neither party's total liability under this Agreement shall exceed **USD $50,000**.

10.2 Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages.

## 11. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

11.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the **Hong Kong Special Administrative Region**.

11.2 Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be submitted to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Hong Kong.

## 12. MISCELLANEOUS

12.1 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the Employment Agreement and Exhibit A, constitutes the entire understanding between the parties regarding intellectual property assignment.

12.2 **Amendments.** Any amendment must be in writing and signed by both parties.

12.3 **Severability.** If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force.

12.4 **Waiver.** Failure to enforce any right or provision shall not constitute a waiver.

12.5 **Assignment.** Employee may not assign this Agreement. Company may assign this Agreement in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets.

12.6 **Survival.** Sections 2 (Definition), 3 (Assignment), 4 (Disclosure), 6 (Confidentiality), 7 (Return), 9 (Remedies), 10 (Liability), 11 (Governing Law), and 12 (Miscellaneous) shall survive termination of employment.
